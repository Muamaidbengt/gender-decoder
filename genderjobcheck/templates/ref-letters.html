Check reference letters for subtle bias

Without realising it, we all use language that is subtly ‘gender-coded’. Society has certain expectations of what men and women are like, and how they differ, and this seeps into the language we use.

Research has shown that this language can creep into reference letters. Women are less likely to be described as brilliant than men and, even if they are, more likely to have their success credited hard work rather than natural talent.*
